Graduate Courses

Theory of Elasticity
  • Applying fundamental principles and solution techniques used in the stress analysis of elastic solids and structures
Finite Element Method in Aerospace Structures
  • Understand the theory behind finite element calculations of stress, strain, and deformation in structures and materials
Mechanics of Composite Materials
  • Transform material properties between various coordinate systems. Compute effective properties of composites. Predict displacement, stress, strain, strength and failure of lamina and laminates under mechanical and thermal loads
Manufacturing of Advanced Composite Materials
  • Examine the phenomena involved in the net-shaped manufacturing of fiber reinforced polymer composite structures such as: Darcy flow, permeability and compaction of fiber beds, thermoset reaction kinetics, polymer viscosity, heat transfer, void formation, polymer physical properties, and anisotropic composite properties
Dynamics & Stability in Aerospace Structures
  • Conceptual understanding of problems in the dynamics and stability of continuous lightweight structural systems and of the relevant analytical tools regarding general structural principles, Ritz method for discrete approximations, modal analysis, nonlinear vibrations, and stability of motion
Micromechanics
  • Analyze problems in micromechanics and interpret technical literature relevant to micromechanics. Comprehensive understanding of the methods for the analysis and prediction of properties of micro-structured materials
Characterization of Advanced Composite Materials
  • Familiar with composite microstructural characterization, lamina longitudinal, shear, off-axis, flexural, thermoelastic and compression tests, laminate tensile, thermoelastic, notch strength, mode I and II interlaminar fracture, stacking sequence and compression tests
Multiscale Structural Mechanics
  • Capable of identifying distinct features of composites and challenges associated with modeling and simulation of composites. Perform critical evaluation of major theories in the literature regarding their strengths and weaknesses
  • Capable of examining and explaining simulation results for composite structures. Able to apply mechanics of structure genome to derive new models for composite structures
  • Capable of designing and analyzing composite structures using SwiftComp and ABAQUS/Standard
Design Theory and Methods for Aerospace Systems
  • Generated system-level requirements for aerospace systems. Applied design methods and tools to create conceptual designs of aerospace systems, assessed design against requirements, and performed trade-off studies
Statistical Methods
  • Familiar with statistical methods and tools such as hypothesis testing, two sample inference, analysis of variance (ANOVA), multi-factor analysis of Variance, and simple linear regression
Applied Regression Analysis
  • Simple linear regression (SLR), statistical inference for the slope and intercept in SLR, interval estimation of mean response, confidence band for regression line, ANOVA approach to regression analysis, Pearson correlation and R2, F-test for lack of fit, Diagnostics in SLR, remedial procedure in SLR transformation, simultaneous confidence inference in SLR, linear regression in matrix form
  • Multiple linear regression (MLR) introduction & prediction, extra sums of squares in MLR, MLR partial correlation, MLR multicollinearity, MLR polynomial, MLR qualitative predictors, MLR model selection criterion, MLR automatic model selection, MLR diagnotics, MLR remedy, one-way ANOVA factor effect model, two-way ANOVA factor level means study, unequal cell sample size type III ANOVA, fixed random effects
Systems of Systems Modeling & Analysis

The techniques include optical microscopy, scanning electron microscopy, scanning probe microscopy, electron beam instruments coupled with specific detectors for monitoring particle emission
Magnetic Nanostructures
  • Fundamental understanding of condensed matter behavior of metallic, dielectric, and molecular nature. Fundamental understanding includes electron transport in metals (Drude model), electric field frequency dependence of metallic conductivity and polarization, atomic polarizability (Clausius-Mossotti relation), quantum theory of atomic paramagnetism, molecular field theory, and various quantum mechanical concepts: particle in a well, perturbation theory, band formation, and tight-binding model. Interpreting magnetic characterization methods such as the neutron diffraction for crystalline materials
Fluid Mechanics and Transport Processes

Mechanics of Nanomaterials

Electronic, Optical and Magnetic Properties of Nanomaterials

Thermodynamics and Kinetics of Nanomaterials
